Beyond basic OCR

OCR converts images into text. Document intelligence adds classification, field extraction, semantic understanding, relationship detection, validation, and workflow routing.

The processing pipeline

A robust pipeline ingests a document, identifies its type, extracts text and layout, captures relevant fields, evaluates confidence, validates against rules or systems, and routes exceptions for review.

Context matters

The same number or clause can mean different things depending on the document, section, party, period, and process. Enterprise-grade systems retain that context rather than returning isolated text.

Human-in-the-loop review

Low-confidence fields, conflicting information, missing pages, unusual clauses, or policy exceptions should be presented to the right reviewer with the source evidence visible.

Operational use cases

Examples include tax document collection, insurance submissions, legal matter intake, contract review, invoice processing, claims documentation, onboarding records, and compliance evidence.

Security and retention

Document intelligence should honor access controls, encryption, retention schedules, legal holds, redaction rules, and audit requirements throughout the document lifecycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between OCR and document intelligence?

OCR recognizes characters. Document intelligence interprets document structure and meaning, validates extracted information, and triggers business actions.

Can it process handwritten or scanned files?

Capability depends on image quality, handwriting clarity, language, and model support. Confidence scoring and human review remain important.

How is accuracy improved?

Accuracy improves through document-specific extraction schemas, validation rules, reference data, confidence thresholds, and feedback from reviewed exceptions.
