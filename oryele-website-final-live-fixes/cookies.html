Cookie Policy

Effective July 14, 2026 · Last updated July 14, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Oryele LLC uses cookies and similar technologies on the public Oryele website.

1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files stored on a device when a website is visited. Similar technologies may include local storage, session identifiers, pixels, and other mechanisms supporting website functionality, security, preferences, and measurement.

2. Strictly necessary technologies

Oryele may use essential technologies for secure page delivery, traffic management, fraud prevention, form protection, preference storage, and other functions required to operate the website. Blocking essential technologies may prevent parts of the site from working correctly.

3. Analytics and performance

Oryele may use analytics or performance technologies when those services are enabled and, where legally required, after consent. These technologies help us understand aggregate usage, page performance, referral sources, and errors. Oryele does not use website analytics cookies to sell personal information.

4. Third-party services

Website functions may be provided by hosting, content delivery, form-processing, embedded-media, security, or analytics providers. A provider may receive technical information or set cookies according to its own privacy notice. The providers actually enabled may change as the website is updated.

5. Current public-site position

The public Oryele website is designed to operate primarily with essential technologies. If non-essential analytics, advertising, or embedded-media cookies are introduced, Oryele will update this Policy and provide a preference mechanism where required.

6. Managing cookies

You can delete or block cookies through your browser settings. Where a cookie preference control is displayed, you may use it to change non-essential choices. Browser controls vary by provider and device.

7. Privacy signals

Browser-based signals are not interpreted uniformly across the industry. Where applicable law requires recognition of a supported opt-out preference signal, Oryele will process that signal according to the law and technologies enabled on the website.

8. Changes

We may update this Policy when technologies, providers, or legal requirements change. The “Last updated” date identifies the current version.

Questions may be sent to info@oryele.com or Oryele LLC, 1021 Broad St. #1129, Shrewsbury, NJ 07702, United States.
