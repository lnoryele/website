1. Purpose of this policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Oryele LLC ("Oryele," "we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on oryele.ai and related web properties.

2. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files stored on a device when a website is visited. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels, tags, and scripts.

3. Categories of cookies we may use

  • Strictly necessary cookies: Required for core website functions, security, session management, fraud prevention, and delivery of requested services.
  • Preference cookies: Remember settings and choices, including display or accessibility preferences.
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand website usage, performance, and errors so we can improve the site.
  • Marketing cookies: We do not use cookies to sell personal information or share SMS consent data for third-party marketing. If marketing technologies are introduced, this policy and any applicable consent controls will be updated before use.

4. Third-party services

Our website may rely on service providers for hosting, security, content delivery, analytics, or embedded content. Those providers may set or read cookies according to their own privacy practices and only as permitted by applicable law and our agreements with them.

5. Cookie duration

Session cookies expire when the browser is closed. Persistent cookies remain until their stated expiration date or until they are deleted. Retention periods vary based on each cookie's purpose.

6. Your choices

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Blocking strictly necessary cookies may prevent parts of the website from working correctly. Where required by law, we will present a consent mechanism before using non-essential cookies.

7. Privacy signals

Browser-based privacy signals are handled in accordance with applicable law. Because standards continue to evolve, responses may vary by browser and jurisdiction.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in technology, law, or our practices. The "Last updated" date above identifies the current version.
