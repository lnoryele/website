Privacy Policy

Oryele LLC  ·  1021 Broad St. #1129, Shrewsbury, NJ 07702  ·  Effective date: June 22, 2026  ·  Last updated: July 16, 2026

1. Who we are

Oryele LLC ("Oryele", "we", "us", or "our") is a technology company headquartered at 1021 Broad St. #1129, Shrewsbury, NJ 07702. We provide an AI-powered digital workforce platform for professional service organizations, including accounting firms, law firms, and insurance organizations. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information in connection with our platform, website, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

2. Information we collect

We collect information in the following ways:

  • Information you provide directly — such as your name, company name, email address, phone number, and business information when you register, request a briefing, or contact us.
  • Client and end-user information — when accounting firms and other customers ("Clients") use our platform, they may submit information about their own clients and personnel. We process this information on behalf of our Clients as a data processor.
  • Usage and technical data — including IP addresses, browser type, pages visited, referring URLs, and device information collected automatically when you use our Services.
  • Communications — including emails, SMS messages, and voice call records processed through our platform in the ordinary course of delivering our Services.

3. How we use information

We use collected information to:

  • Provide, operate, and improve our platform and Services
  • Send transactional communications including appointment confirmations, reminders, document requests, and notifications on behalf of our Clients
  • Respond to inquiries and provide customer support
  • Ensure the security and integrity of our platform
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations
  • Analyze usage to improve our Services

4. SMS and voice communications

Oryele may send SMS notifications and voice communications on behalf of Clients to their authorized end users. These messages are transactional and service-related, including appointment reminders, scheduling confirmations, document requests, workflow notifications, engagement or case status updates, and account-related service notices.

SMS consent is optional. End users opt in through the Oryele Client Portal during onboarding by manually selecting an unchecked SMS consent checkbox. Users are not automatically enrolled, and consent is not a condition of purchasing goods or services.

Oryele does not sell SMS consent information. SMS opt-in data and phone numbers are not shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes.

Message frequency varies based on appointment schedules, document requests, and workflow activity. Message and data rates may apply. To opt out of SMS communications, reply STOP to any message. For assistance, reply HELP or contact support@oryele.com.

For additional messaging details and a public example of our opt-in workflow, please see our SMS Policy.

5. How we share information

We do not sell personal information. We may share information in the following limited circumstances:

  • With our Clients — we share information as directed by the Client on whose behalf we are processing data
  • With service providers — we engage trusted third-party vendors (including cloud infrastructure, communication, and analytics providers) who process data on our behalf under appropriate data processing agreements
  • For legal compliance — we may disclose information when required by law, regulation, or valid legal process
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, information may be transferred as part of that transaction

6. Data security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information, including AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS encryption in transit. All client documents and sensitive data are stored on AWS S3 with encryption enabled. Access to personal information is restricted to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis. We maintain comprehensive audit logs and employ multi-factor authentication across our systems.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we take reasonable precautions, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our Services, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Clients may request deletion of their data in accordance with our customer agreements.

8. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal information, including the right to access, correct, or delete information we hold about you. To exercise these rights, please contact us at the address below.

California residents may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA.

9. Cookies and tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to improve functionality and analyze usage. You may configure your browser to refuse cookies, though some features of our website may not function correctly as a result.

10. Third-party links

Our website and platform may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their privacy policies.

11. Children's privacy

Our Services are not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the updated policy on this page with a revised effective date. Your continued use of our Services after any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
